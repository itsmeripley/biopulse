Migration
Rose federation explained

What is Rose Federation?

Rose Federation is a built-in feature of Rose Bot that links multiple groups together for moderation purposes. Without it, banning a bad actor in Group 1 does nothing to stop them joining Group 2, 3, or 4. With Federation active, one ban covers the entire community instantly — no admin needs to manually action it in every group.

01
Create the Federation
One admin creates a named Federation using a simple Rose command. Takes under a minute.
02
Link all groups
Each subgroup is joined to the Federation. Rose handles the rest automatically across all linked chats.
03
Ban once, protect all
Any federation-level ban instantly removes and blocks the user from every linked group simultaneously.
Moderation playbook
Rose Bot — settings in every group
Captcha on — every new joiner must verify before they can post
Link blocking — members cannot post external links or invite URLs
Flood control — automatic mute after 5 rapid messages
Warning system — 3 warnings triggers an automatic ban
Welcome message — automated greeting with group rules on join
Admin responsibilities
One lead moderator per group — clear ownership, no confusion
Consistent rules — same ruleset pinned in every group, no variation
Federation bans only — always ban at federation level, never just group level
No cross-group links — members must not be able to find other subgroups
Broadcast channel only — all official posts go through the channel, never directly into groups
Migration deadline plan
1
Week 1
Announce the move
Post in the existing group explaining the new structure, why it is happening, and what members need to do. Pin the message. Give a clear deadline date.
2
Week 1–2
Distribute invite links in batches
Send individual invite links to members in controlled batches of 100–150. Each batch goes to a different subgroup. Track who has moved across.
3
Week 2
Set the old group to read-only
Remove posting rights from all members in the old group. Pin a final message with the deadline and the action required. Admins only can still post reminders.
4
Week 3
Close the old group
On the agreed deadline, the old group is archived or deleted. No exceptions. A hard deadline is the only way to ensure a clean migration with no stragglers.
What we are not building — and why

Keeping this realistic and deliverable

Some suggestions in the original brief describe developer-level infrastructure that is unnecessary at this scale and would require significant ongoing maintenance. The following items are out of scope — and the community will run perfectly well without them.

Custom distribution bot
Requires a developer, a hosted server, ongoing maintenance, and costs money. Completely unnecessary for a 500-person community.
A free off-the-shelf forwarding bot does exactly the same job in minutes with no code and no server.
Delivery logs and retry queues
An ops-level monitoring system built for enterprise platforms. Overkill by a significant margin for this use case.
If a message fails to forward, an admin simply reposts it. This will be a rare edge case, not a daily problem.
Separate admin monitoring bot
Rose Bot already covers moderation logging, warnings, and ban tracking across all groups via Federation.
Rose Federation gives admins a unified view of bans and moderation actions without any extra tooling.
Join spike and spam spike alerts
At 500 members across 5 groups, admins will naturally notice unusual activity. Automated alerting is not needed at this scale.
Rose flood control and captcha screening will catch and suppress the vast majority of spam automatically.
Scaling as the community grows

The structure is built to grow. As membership increases, new subgroups are simply added and connected to the existing broadcast and Federation. No rebuild required — just open a new group, add the forwarding bot, link it to the Federation, and point new joiners there.

How new joiners are directed automatically
When a new group is needed, a fresh invite link is generated and shared with incoming members only. Existing members stay in their current group. The broadcast channel and Rose Federation both update in minutes — add the new group to the forwarding bot and the Federation, and it is live. No disruption to existing members whatsoever.
Do we need Telegram Premium?
No — not at any stage of this plan. All group and channel features used here have always been free on Telegram and remain free. The only limit worth being aware of is that one free account can create up to 10 public groups. Since our subgroups are private, this is largely a non-issue — and at any stage where more groups are needed, spreading creation across two or three admin accounts costs nothing and takes minutes. Premium at £4.99/month per account is available if needed later, but there is no requirement to spend anything to build or scale this community.
